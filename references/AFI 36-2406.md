# AFI 36-2406

Use this reference for officer and enlisted performance-report writing.

Source reviewed: `AFI 36-2406, Officer and Enlisted Evaluations Systems`, as modified by `AFI36-2406_AFGM2026-01`, dated `25 February 2026`.

## What this policy controls for this skill

- Officer and enlisted performance-brief structure.
- Section-based evaluation writing rules.
- Mandatory fitness-comment requirements.
- Stratification and future-role guardrails.
- Plain-language and approved-acronym expectations for duty descriptions and evaluation statements.

## Performance-report lane versus awards and decorations

- Performance reports are not citation prose and not `AF Form 1206` blocks.
- The writing is organized by required sections on the evaluation, not by freeform accomplishment order alone.
- Comments are narrative performance statements inside specific sections.

## Duty description rules

- Duty descriptions must reflect the uniqueness of the ratee's job.
- Be specific about scope, people supervised, resources managed, and level of responsibility.
- Use plain English.
- Avoid jargon and topical references that obscure meaning.
- Only acronyms on the approved acronym list are authorized.
- Previous jobs during the reporting period should be mentioned only if they materially affect the evaluation.

## Section-writing rules

- Required sections must contain at least one performance statement unless the form specifically allows `THIS SECTION NOT USED`.
- Officer and enlisted briefs both use sectioned performance writing rather than a single combined narrative.
- Keep content aligned to the intended section instead of mixing mission, leadership, resource management, and unit-improvement material together.

## Fitness mandatory comments

- Performance reports must document the member's most recent and current official fitness assessment as of the closeout date.
- Use the required format for fitness comments.
- If the member is not current, use `PFA: Required - Not Current`.
- For full exemptions, use only the authorized exemption terms and do not add medical narrative.
- For designated combat-related AFSCs, document both `PFA` and `CFT` results when required.
- Do not delay signing an evaluation to wait for a later fitness test after the closeout date.

## Stratification and future-role guardrails

- Stratification must follow the policy's authorized formats and peer-group rules.
- If no stratification is used in a stratification block, use the prescribed `THIS SECTION NOT USED` language when required by the form instructions.
- Future roles may not be used as veiled promotion statements.
- Recommend only roles the member is eligible for based on current grade, projected grade, or authorized promotion-eligibility rules at the evaluation closeout.

## Practical takeaway for this skill

- When drafting a performance report, first map each accomplishment to the correct evaluation section.
- Keep the language factual, scoped, and readable in plain English.
- Preserve mandatory-comment compliance separately from performance advocacy.
- Do not import decoration rhetoric or `1206` compression patterns into the evaluation unless the form's section and space constraints clearly support it.
