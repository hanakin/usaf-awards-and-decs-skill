# DAFPD 36-28

Use this reference as the policy foundation for the Department of the Air Force awards program.

Source reviewed: `DAFPD 36-28, Awards Program, 24 May 2021, incorporating Change 1 certified current 2 June 2023`.

## What it establishes

- The Department of the Air Force maintains an awards program to recognize bravery, outstanding achievement, and meritorious service.
- The program also covers special trophies and awards, plus campaign, expeditionary, service, and achievement medals.
- The policy applies across the Department of the Air Force, including uniformed and civilian personnel in the Regular Air Force, Regular Space Force, Air Force Reserve, and Air National Guard.

## How to use it in this skill

- Treat this document as top-level policy background, not as the detailed writing guide for bullets, citations, or AF Form 1206 content.
- Use it to anchor scope, authority, and purpose when a user asks what the awards program covers or which population it applies to.
- Defer detailed drafting and process questions to lower-level procedural publications when those are provided.

## Constraints and cautions

- The directive states that it may not be supplemented.
- Do not invent local policy rules that conflict with this publication or other higher-level guidance.
- Local templates may still control package format, routing artifacts, and presentation when they do not conflict with governing policy.

## Authority and ownership

- `SAF/MR` provides guidance, direction, and oversight for DAF awards policy.
- `AF/A1` develops and executes procedural guidance for USAF awards.
- `SF/S1` develops and executes procedural guidance for USSF awards.

## Operational takeaway

- When drafting awards content, use this reference to confirm the awards program exists to recognize meaningful acts, achievements, and service.
- Do not rely on this directive alone for package mechanics, formatting rules, or nomination-writing details.
