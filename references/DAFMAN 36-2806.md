# DAFMAN 36-2806

Use this reference for detailed military awards criteria, recommendation mechanics, and citation rules.

Source reviewed: `DAFMAN 36-2806, Military Awards Criteria and Procedures, 27 October 2022, incorporating Change 3 certified current 13 August 2025`.

## What this manual controls

- Detailed criteria and processing procedures for Department of the Air Force military awards.
- Personal military decoration basis, eligibility, recommendation timing, inclusive dates, and approval flow.
- Citation preparation rules, formatting limits, and required opening and closing sentence patterns.

## High-value drafting rules to apply

- Recommendations should not read like automatic end-of-tour recognition. The justification must show why the member clearly exceeded normal duty expectations.
- Narrative justification must be factual, concrete, and verifiable. Avoid vague praise, broad generalities, and unsupported superlatives.
- Duplicate recognition is restricted. Do not reuse the same act, achievement, or service to justify overlapping decorations.
- Meritorious service and outstanding achievement are distinct bases. Use the right basis and period for the requested decoration.

## Recommendation mechanics

- Submit recommendations as soon as possible after the act, achievement, or service.
- Recommendations are prepared in chronological sequence unless wartime or intense operational conditions delayed processing.
- `MSM`, `AAM`, `ASCOM`, and `ASAM` are prepared and submitted in `myDecs`.
- Other decorations use `DECOR6` plus supporting documents as required.
- Narrative justification is optional for `LOM`, `MSM`, `ASCOM`, and `ASAM` in some cases, but required for exception-to-policy packages and some combat-related matters.
- When a narrative justification is used, it should be one page unless a specific exception applies.
- Proposed citations are required for recommendations except the `Purple Heart`.
- Stratification statements are not authorized.

## Citation rules

- Citation structure is opening sentence, narrative description, and closing sentence.
- Every citation needs at least one complete sentence in the narrative description.
- `MSM`, `AM`, `AAM`, `ASCOM`, and `ASAM` citations are limited to `1350` characters.
- `BSM` and higher citations are limited to `16` lines.
- Use `Courier New` 11 for `MSM`, `AM`, `AAM`, `ASCOM`, and `ASAM`.
- Use `Courier New` 11 or 12 for `BSM` and higher.
- Keep the inclusive period on the same line.
- Spell out numbers through nine unless space constraints make that impractical.
- Do not include classified information.
- Do not use superfluous embellishments or quotations.

## Acronyms and abbreviations

- Citations may use only authorized DAF abbreviations and acronyms.
- Commonly identified operation or exercise names may be used.
- In citation text, avoid abbreviations other than approved forms like suffixes (`Jr.`, `Sr.`, `II`, `III`) and approved DAF items.
- Spell out compound grades in the opening sentence, then use the short title later in the citation.
- Numeric unit designators should appear as `3rd Aerospace Wing`, `4th Mission Support Group`, and similar forms.

## Retirement wording rule

- For retirement decoration closing sentences, use `long and distinguished career` only when the member has 30 years or more of qualifying service.

## Practical takeaway for this skill

- Use this reference as the main source for military-decoration drafting constraints.
- When user input conflicts with this manual, preserve the user facts but align the packaging, basis, wording, and citation mechanics to this manual unless a later controlling source says otherwise.
- If a user asks for a citation, check award-specific opening and closing patterns here before finalizing the draft.
