# DAFI 36-2803

Use this reference for Department of the Air Force military decorations and awards program governance.

Source reviewed: `DAFI 36-2803, Military Decorations and Awards Program, 3 May 2022, incorporating Change 3 certified current 28 January 2026`.

## What this instruction controls

- Program-level rules for DAF military decorations and awards.
- Approval-authority structure and device authorization framework.
- Guardrails on duplicate recognition, acronym use, joint-award routing, and when lesser recognition should be used instead of a decoration.

## High-value rules for this skill

- Recommendations must be entered into official channels within `3` years and awarded within `5` years of the act, achievement, or service.
- Only one personal military decoration or award of the same type is authorized for the same act, achievement, or period of service.
- Recommendations should not become routine expectations at promotion, PCS, PCA, transfer, or retirement milestones unless the performance actually merits the recognition.
- Academic honors, inspection recognition, quarterly or annual recognition, and similar achievements do not by themselves justify a personal military decoration.
- Do not use decorations as incentives, prizes, or rewards for voluntary-program participation.

## Acronym rule

- Acronyms in award nominations should be minimized.
- Limit acronym use to commonly known acronyms and unit or functional designators unless a later controlling source authorizes more.

## Joint and non-DAF context

- For members assigned to defense or joint organizations, the default recognition is the appropriate DoD joint award.
- DAF awards are generally reserved in that context for retirement or separation when the recognized service was DAF-specific, or for combat conditions when an appropriate DoD joint award does not exist.

## Approval and device context

- `SecAF` retains top-level approval authority and delegates specific authorities through the instruction.
- Approval authority is position-based, not a function of "senior rater" status or informal standing.
- `V` and `C` device authority is specifically controlled.
- Use this instruction to sanity-check whether the requested decoration, device, or routing path is plausible before drafting.

## Honorable service rule

- No military decoration or associated device may be awarded or presented if the individual's service during or after the recognized act, achievement, or service was not honorable.

## Other-recognition fallback

- If the facts do not support a military decoration, consider whether the better fit is another recognition product such as a certificate of commendation, certificate of achievement, or one-time recognition item.
- This matters when the user is trying to recognize solid performance that does not meet decoration-level standards.

## Practical takeaway for this skill

- Use this reference to decide whether a decoration is appropriate at all, whether the request looks routine or inflated, and whether a joint-award or lower-recognition path is more defensible.
- Use `DAFMAN 36-2806` for the detailed writing and citation mechanics after this instruction confirms the recognition path is appropriate.
